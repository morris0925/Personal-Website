About

Systems that tell you why, not just what.

I'm Morris — I build the pipelines that move data and the layer on top that explains what happened in it. That split shows up in almost everything I've worked on: an ETL system is only as useful as the dashboard that surfaces its gaps, and a stream is only as useful as the anomaly it can name.

Before I was writing Kafka consumers, I was doing product strategy at Yahoo and business transformation at Delta Electronics — reading through user interviews and KPI dashboards to find the one gap worth fixing. That habit of asking "what does this data actually mean" is the throughline from product work into backend engineering, and into the research I did on corporate disclosure at NTU.

This fall I start a Master of Applied Science in Computer Science at Penn, after finishing a B.B.A. in Operations and Data Analytics at National Taiwan University. I'm looking for data engineering and backend infrastructure internships for Summer 2027.
