The problem

Scheduling a group is a coordination tax

Every group event starts the same way: someone screenshots their calendar, someone else types out "I'm free Tues after 6," and a thread spends three days converging on a time a poll could have found in three minutes. The friction isn't the poll — it's translating everyone's actual availability into a format a poll can use.

The approach

Let people show up as they are

Down accepts a calendar screenshot or a sentence like "free after 6 except Thursday" and an LLM turns either into structured availability. The app proposes times from the overlap, but the group makes the final call — it removes the busywork of collecting availability, not the decision itself.

What's next

Google Calendar, then the rest of the stack

Interviews from the first cohort of users pointed at one integration above all others: pulling availability straight from Google Calendar instead of a screenshot. That's the next build — followed by the other calendar providers people actually use.
